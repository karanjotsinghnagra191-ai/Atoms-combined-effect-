# Atoms-combined-effect-
This theory suggests that when atoms come close, interactions between their internal charges create attraction. The combined effect of many atoms may appear as gravity at a larger scale.
This theory proposes a new perspective on gravity by suggesting that it may originate from interactions at the atomic level. Traditionally, gravity is understood as an attractive force between masses, but this idea explores the possibility that its origin lies in microscopic processes. Each atom contains charged particles such as electrons and protons, which interact through electromagnetic forces. When atoms come close to each other, these internal charges create attractive interactions. While such effects are typically associated with chemical bonding, this theory suggests that when a very large number of atoms interact simultaneously, their combined effect may manifest as gravity on a macroscopic scale. In this view, large objects such as planets can be considered as systems composed of countless interacting atoms, whose collective behavior produces a unified attractive influence. Therefore, gravity may not be entirely independent but could partially emerge from cumulative charge-based interactions. Although this idea is still in an early conceptual stage and requires mathematical formulation and experimental validation, it offers a new direction for understanding the connection between microscopic interactions and large-scale gravitational phenomena.
